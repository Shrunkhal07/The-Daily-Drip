India's Quick Commerce War Has a New Battleground ‚Äî and It's Not Groceries

As Blinkit, Zepto, and Swiggy Instamart compete for dominance, a quiet pivot into electronics, fashion, and beauty is reshaping the economics of 10-minute delivery.

When Albinder Dhindsa, CEO of Blinkit, casually mentioned on an earnings call that "grocery is just the starting point," most analysts brushed it off as corporate posturing. Three months later, Blinkit is delivering laptops in 12 minutes. The quick commerce war has entered a new phase.

India's quick commerce market ‚Äî valued at $5.5 billion in FY25 ‚Äî was built on the promise of instant groceries. Milk, bread, vegetables ‚Äî the staples that every household needs, delivered in under 15 minutes. It was a logistics marvel, powered by dark stores, hyperlocal warehousing, and an army of delivery partners navigating India's chaotic streets.

But grocery, it turns out, is a terrible business. Margins are razor-thin. Spoilage rates hover around 8-12%. And the average order value sits at a stubbornly low ‚Çπ350-500. To make the unit economics work, you need enormous scale ‚Äî or you need to sell something else entirely.

The Electronics Pivot

Swiggy Instamart fired the first shot in December 2025, quietly listing iPhones, AirPods, and Samsung Galaxy phones in select Bengaluru and Mumbai dark stores. The results were startling: electronics orders, while comprising just 3% of total volume, accounted for nearly 18% of gross margin contribution in the pilot markets.

The math is simple. A single iPhone sale at ‚Çπ79,900 generates more margin than approximately 200 grocery orders. The delivery cost is identical ‚Äî one rider, one trip, ten minutes. But the value captured per delivery is astronomically higher.

"We realized our dark stores are essentially the most efficient retail real estate in India. Why limit them to onions and milk?" ‚Äî Senior VP at Swiggy Instamart, speaking on condition of anonymity

Blinkit followed within weeks, launching an "Electronics Express" category across Delhi-NCR. Zepto, not to be outdone, partnered with Nykaa for premium beauty products and with Croma for select electronics ‚Äî creating a marketplace-within-a-marketplace model.

The Dark Store as Premium Retail

What makes this pivot possible is the evolution of the dark store itself. First-generation dark stores were essentially converted garages ‚Äî utilitarian spaces optimized for speed. The new generation looks different: climate-controlled zones for electronics, secure storage for high-value items, and even dedicated packaging stations.

The cost? Surprisingly manageable. Adding an electronics zone to an existing dark store costs ‚Çπ3-5 lakh in one-time setup, with inventory funded largely through brand partnerships and consignment models. The real estate is already paid for. The delivery fleet already exists. The customer already has the app.

What This Means for E-Commerce

The implications for Flipkart and Amazon are significant. Quick commerce's expansion into electronics doesn't threaten their core catalog ‚Äî nobody's buying a washing machine on Blinkit. But for impulse purchases in the ‚Çπ1,000-80,000 range ‚Äî earphones, smartwatches, phone cases, beauty devices, premium skincare ‚Äî the 10-minute promise is a powerful draw.

Early data suggests that quick commerce electronics buyers skew younger (22-30), urban, and affluent ‚Äî precisely the demographic that e-commerce platforms fight hardest to retain.

The Risks

Not everything about this pivot is rosy. Electronics carry different risks than groceries: higher return rates (estimated 8-10% vs. 2% for grocery), warranty and service obligations, and the capital intensity of stocking high-value inventory.

There's also the regulatory question. Current quick commerce regulations were designed for essential goods. Premium electronics delivery may attract different GST treatment, consumer protection requirements, and return policies.

But the direction is clear. Quick commerce in India is evolving from a grocery delivery service into something more ambitious: a platform for instant gratification across every product category. The question isn't whether this transformation will happen ‚Äî it's who will lead it.
